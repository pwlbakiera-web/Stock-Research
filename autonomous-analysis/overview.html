Input: ticker Output: report + verdict BUY / WAIT / PASS 4 modules: M3 · M1 · M2 · M4 valuation engine: pure Python
01

What it actually is

Picture a fund team or a newsroom. You throw in a company name and do nothing else. The machine, on its own: sends reporters out for the facts, has an accountant compute what the company is worth, an editor write the piece with a verdict, and then a devil's advocate tries to tear that piece apart. At the end the editor returns and tempers the thesis under fire – so that only the one that survived the attack reaches you. The result: a finished report with a clear "buy / wait / pass" and the risks spelled out.

How the analysis flows

A production line for analysis – from ticker to finished report, in 6 stages. Each block takes the previous one's output. Badges on the right: who computes (model) and status (✓ done).

START

Ticker + date

You give only the company symbol and a cutoff date. The rest happens on its own.

▼ the symbol goes to ingest
M3 · step 1

Ingest – gathering the raw material

✓ done

First a fact-checker acquires the hard data, then 6 reporters in parallel study the company from 6 angles.

S0 · data-pack opus Goes first and alone. Collects numbers (price, debt, FCF, revenue), sources, and sets the market regime (cycle or durable growth). Everything else stands on this data.
S1business + technology, explained in plain terms
S2competitive advantage (moat) and its direction
S3risks and thesis-breakers
S4growth, market size (TAM), levers
S5outlook + guidance, market forecasts, everything that reinforces and breaks the thesis (new markets, threats) + narrative evolution
S6sentiment, insiders, management

S1–S6 = sonnet  ·  they run at once (parallel), each with a narrow brief

▼ the hard numbers (S0) go to valuation
M1 · step 2

Valuation – the calculator

python✓ done

Zero AI, pure math – the same data always gives the same result, no number can be "invented". It computes what the company is worth, in one of two modes:

mode A · reverse-DCF The company earns. It asks: what growth is the market already pricing in, and what is real? The gap = the opportunity.
mode B · pre-profit / Fermi The company does not earn yet (e.g. a crypto miner). Valued via mature margin, power (MW), ARR.

output: a scenario fan (bear / base / bull) + value per share + a scale sanity-check

▼ numbers + research go to the editor
M4 · step 3

Synthesis – the editor writes the thesis

opus✓ done

The heart of M4. One editor takes EVERYTHING at once (data + valuation + 6 streams) and writes a coherent "doctrine verdict": a thesis, a recommendation (BUY / WAIT / PASS + conviction) and the same 10 fields. One brain = no internal contradictions.

1 · edgewhat the market models wrong
2 · state of knowledgewhat's certain, what's a guess
3 · hypothesiswhat must be true
5 · implied vs actualvaluation vs reality
6 · asymmetryupside vs downside
8 · recommendationtranches / wait

+ fields 4 (outside view), 7 (thesis-breakers), 9 (take-profit), 10 (frozen expectation)

▼ the finished draft goes to the firing squad
M2 · step 4

Adversarial attack – the devil's advocate

3× opus✓ done

Three devil's advocates attack the draft symmetrically: one tears the bull thesis apart, another the bear thesis, the third audits the doctrine gates. It repeats until there are no new charges – only a thesis that survived the barrage reaches you.

attack the bullmandate: tear apart the long thesis
attack the bearmandate: tear apart the "wait / it will fall" thesis
gate audit 1–5did the run break the rules?
⟳ loop-until-dry · repeat rounds until 0 new charges, max 3

returns a list of holes (findings), does not rewrite the report – the revision does that. Unresolved ones → explicitly flagged in the report.

▼ the holes + draft go back to the editor
M4 · step 5

Revision – tempering the thesis

opus✓ done

The editor takes the draft + the charges from the attack and writes the FINAL REPORT. It does not apply the criticism blindly – it weighs each one.

folds infixes where the charge is valid
defendsrefutes with evidence where the charge is wrong
resolvesreconciles contradictions (e.g. a verdict divergence)

what cannot be resolved → lands as an explicit flag in the "open risks" section. The verdict can change if the attack landed.

▼ the finished report goes to be saved
M4 · step 6

Save – to the archive

✓ done

The finished report, the data pack and the verdict card land in the company's archive (your research vault) – as a durable record you can return to.

03

Who's who (in plain terms)

The same modules, described by team roles.

R

Reporters – M3 / S1-S6

sonnet · 6 in parallel

Each digs into one topic: how the business works, whether it has a moat, what can go wrong, where growth comes from, how management shifted its tone, what others feel. Fast and narrow.

F

Fact-checker – M3 / S0

opus · goes first

Acquires the hard numbers and sources. If something is missing – it marks "est." and lowers confidence, never invents. The whole rest stands on its data (the GIGO rule).

A

Accountant – M1

Python · zero AI

A pure calculator. Takes the numbers from the fact-checker and computes the company's value – in a mode for earners or for not-yet-earners. Deterministic: always the same result.

D

Devil's advocate – M2

3× opus · loops to exhaustion

The most important quality filter. Three at once attack the finished thesis evenly from both sides and enforce the 5 doctrine gates. Only what survives the barrage reaches you.

E

Editor – M4 synthesis + revision

opus · one brain

Writes the thesis from the whole material, and after the attack assembles the final report: folds in valid fixes, defends with evidence where a charge is wrong, and explicitly flags doubts it cannot resolve. One mind = a coherent verdict.

04

The two principles M4 stands on

Why the synthesis and the attack are ordered exactly this way – and not otherwise.

1

One brain for synthesis

consistency above all

The thesis is written by one editor (opus), not a team split across 10 fields. Reason: the verdict must be internally consistent – the verdict card cannot say something different from the reasoning. And that can only be guaranteed when one mind sees the whole thing at once (data, valuation, all 6 streams). Split the work – you get contradictions at the seams.

2

Build first, then attack

build → red-team → temper

Synthesis builds the thesis, the devil's advocate (M2) fires at it, the revision tempers it. That is exactly what a good investment process looks like: build a thesis, red-team it, only then act. The order is not accidental – you cannot solidly attack something that does not exist yet, and a thesis that has not passed the attack should not reach a decision.

05

Why you can trust the result

Four rules built into the machine – they keep it from lying and from faking precision.

A
GIGO – garbage in, garbage out

Without trusted data we compute nothing. No number → an "est." flag, never an invention. That is why the fact-checker goes first.

B
The false-precision tax

Every figure from a 5+ year horizon gets a † marker – it is a scenario artifact, not a forecast. We do not pretend to know the year 2031.

C
Symmetric attack

The devil's advocate hits the bull and the bear evenly. There is no cheering for the thesis – the thesis must defend itself.

D
Model map

Reasoning and data integrity = Opus. Research = Sonnet. Numbers = Python. Each does what it is best at.

The 5 gates the devil's advocate enforces (M2):

1Market regime named explicitly?
2Mid-cycle not from a reflex?
3Tail a distribution, not a pretext to "wait"?
4Verdict actionable by default?
5Edge (the gap) named?
Refinement (gate 2): cycle normalization is now per-segment.
The machine used to look at a company in a binary way: either "normalize to mid-cycle" or not. That is too coarse. Many companies are a mix – part driven by a structural boom (e.g. AI datacenter demand) and part ordinary cyclical industry. Now each piece is treated separately: the structural-growth segment – no mid-cycle normalization; assume the cycle continues, and ask only how much the market already prices in (the implied-vs-actual gap) plus a scale sanity-check. the cyclical segment – cycle normalization allowed, as before. Treating such a hybrid as binary distorts the valuation – so the machine weights the decision by segment share.
